Hacker News

Top stories

Hacker News is a social news website focusing on computer science and entrepreneurship. It is run by Y Combinator, an American investment fund and startup incubator. In general, content that can be submitted is defined as anything that gratifies one's intellectual curiosity.

Hacker News is powered by Arc, a dialect of Lisp developed by the site's creators. The site favors ranking algorithms that balance story freshness against community voting signal.

Community rules

On-topic: anything that good hackers would find interesting. That includes more than hacking and startups. If you had to reduce it to a sentence, the answer might be: anything that gratifies one's intellectual curiosity.

Off-topic: most stories about politics, or crime, or sports, unless they're evidence of some interesting new phenomenon. Videos of pratfalls or disasters, or cute animal pictures. If they'd cover it on TV news, it's probably off-topic.

How does ranking work?

Stories appear on the front page based on an algorithm that weighs upvotes against time since submission, with a decay factor so the front page reflects current momentum and not purely cumulative support.
